The chance of LPA developing into a tropical depression is not ruled out by PAGASA.
APR 8, 2022 1:05 PM PHT

PAGASA FORECAST

Due to a prolonged low-pressure region imbedded along the intertropical convergence zone, Mindanao, the Visayas, and parts of Luzon are still wet on Friday, April 8. The state meteorological service warned on Friday morning, April 8, that it is not ruling out the likelihood of the low pressure area (LPA) off Mindanao evolving into a tropical depression. For days, the LPA has dumped moderate to heavy rain, particularly in Mindanao and the Visayas. The LPA was stationed 375 kilometers east of Surigao City, remaining entrenched within the intertropical convergence zone, according to the Philippine Atmospheric, Geophysical, and Astronomical Services Administration (PAGASA) in its 11 a.m. report on Friday (ITCZ). On Friday, rain from both the LPA and the ITCZ will continue.

Because the soil in such regions has already been saturated, PAGASA cautioned that isolated floods and rain-induced landslides are expected, especially in areas where it has been raining for days. If the LPA becomes a tropical depression, it will be the Philippines' first tropical cyclone in 2022, and it will be named Agaton.

For Leni-Kiko, 220,000 Kapampangans put on a spectacular spectacle.
APR 9, 2022 11:27 PM PHT
Image

Philippines – Pampanga's most powerful political dynasty and its most well-known and powerful politician have backed Ferdinand "Bongbong" Marcos Jr. and Sara Duterte's Uniteam ticket. On the other hand, 220,000 Kapampangans showed their support for opposition candidate Vice President Leni Robredo and Senator Kiko Pangilinan on the Day of Valor. The Manalakaran Pampanga grand rally for Robredo and Pangilinan, which drew a crowd of 220,000 according to local organizers, lit up the night around the Robinsons Starmills ground in San Fernando, Pampanga, just four days after former President Gloria Arroyo predicted a "landslide" victory for Marcos-Duterte.

Robredo, who was defeated by Marcos in the 2016 vice-presidential election in this state, spoke in conciliatory terms, and not just for the province that had rejected her. While acknowledging the large audience dressed in her signature pink, Vice President Biden emphasized that as president, she will represent people of all political stripes.

“Ang obligasyon namin, dapat mag-lingkod-bayan sa kahit anong kulay. Magiging pangulo hindi lang ng mga naka-pink pero lahat ng mga kulay. Ang ating paniniwala, ‘pag tapos na ang eleksyon, dapat tapos na ang politika,” Robredo said.

Robredo's Pampanga rally, which follows her April 8 rally in Dagupan, which drew a throng of 78,000, bolsters her assertion of a spike in momentum that the recent polls have scarcely caught.
