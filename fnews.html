Facebook shuts down a military-linked environmental misinformation network in Brazil.
APR 8, 2022 11:31 AM PHT
FACEBOOK

On Thursday, April 7, Facebook owner Meta Platforms said that it had taken down a network of social media accounts linked to the Brazilian military that disguised as bogus charities to downplay the consequences of deforestation. Despite the fact that the individuals participating in the network were active military members, Meta's investigation found insufficient evidence to determine whether they were following orders or acting autonomously, according to a source familiar with the situation. Brazil's President Jair Bolsonaro may suffer a setback as a result of Meta's quarterly report. Long a skeptic of environmentalists, the far-right former army captain has sent the armed forces to the Amazon on failed missions to stop the destruction of the world's biggest jungle.

Bolsonaro's criticisms on giant internet corporations, which he accuses of suffocating conservative viewpoints on their platforms, may be fueled by Meta's first takedown of a network focused solely on environmental problems. Bolsonaro and his followers, according to critics, exploit the platforms to promote harmful disinformation that undermines Brazil's democratic institutions.

"In 2021, they built Pages that pretended to be bogus environmental NGOs and activists in the Amazonas area of Brazil. They wrote about deforestation, saying that not all of it is damaging and condemning respectable environmental NGOs who spoke out against it in the Amazon," according to Meta's study. "Our study discovered linkages to persons linked with the Brazilian Military, despite the fact that the people behind (the network) sought to disguise their names and coordination."

Jackson is the first Black woman to be confirmed to the US Supreme Court by the Senate.
APR 8, 2022 8:02 AM PHT
Image

WASHINGTON DC, USA - On Thursday, April 7, the Senate approved Ketanji Brown Jackson as the first Black woman to serve on the Supreme Court, marking a watershed moment for the United States and a success for President Joe Biden, who delivered on a campaign pledge to diversify the federal judiciary. The vote to confirm the 51-year-old federal appellate judge to a lifetime position on the nation's top judicial body was 53-47, with three Republicans joining Biden's fellow Democrats — Susan Collins, Lisa Murkowski, and Mitt Romney. A simple majority was required, since Jackson overcame Republican resistance in a politicized Supreme Court nomination process.
