Residents of Dumaguete explain why they reject the Smart City reclamation.

A fisherman, a fish vendor, and a pedicab driver who was saved by fishing all claim that their livelihoods would be jeopardized if the local government's reclamation project goes forward. Fragante News travelled throughout Dumaguete City's streets and seas asking locals what they felt about the contentious 174-hectare Smart City reclamation project planned by the local government.

The deal between the local government and a private business was planned to be signed in July 2021, however owing to objections, the city council revoked Mayor Felipe Antonio Remollo's signing power in September 2021. While the P23-billion project promises to create more jobs, attract investments, and combat poverty in the coastal city, prominent Dumaguete scientists and academics have warned that it will have a direct impact on four Marine Protected Areas in the city, including the destruction of the habitat of "more than 200 species of fish," which local fishermen rely on for their livelihood and subsistence.

The Department of Health is requesting FDA permission for COVID-19 booster doses for kids aged 12 to 17.

On Friday, April 9, the Department of Health (DOH) announced that it has submitted a request to the Food and Drug Administration (FDA) to alter the COVID-19 vaccine emergency use authorization (EUA) to include booster injections for kids aged 12 to 17. "'Yan ang fourth dosage at booster injections for youngsters, tayo po ay nag sumite na ng request for an amendment to the EUAs for booster doses for 12 to 17. Ang FDA, na maglabas ng revised EUA, pong hinahantay. In a news conference, Health Undersecretary Maria Rosario Vergeire remarked, "Kapag lumabas po ito ay isusumite po natin sa Health Technology Assessment Council para sa kanilang pagsusuri pa."

The DOH is still waiting for clearance of the updated EUA of COVID-19 vaccinations, which includes a fourth dosage for the elderly and immunocompromised, according to the health spokeswoman. (READ: The Department of Health is requesting FDA clearance for a fourth dosage of immunizations for the elderly and immunocompromised.) Currently, the EUA for COVID-19 vaccines in the country only allows for the administration of primary doses and booster doses to the general public, as well as third injections to the elderly and immunocompromised who have not gained sufficient protection despite their original immunization.
